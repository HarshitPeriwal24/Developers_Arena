07 Key Insights & Findings

Eight evidence-backed conclusions drawn from the statistical analysis.

🌡

Accelerating Warming

Global temperatures rose +1.30°C since 1960, with the warming rate itself increasing post-1990. The 2010s averaged 3.3× warmer than the 1960s baseline.

📈

Near-Perfect CO₂–Temperature Link

Pearson r = 0.980 between CO₂ and temperature anomaly. Both OLS regressions yield R² > 0.93 — among the strongest signals in Earth science.

🧊

Sea Ice in Freefall

September Arctic sea-ice extent has declined by 4.1 million km² — an area larger than the Indian subcontinent — with R² = 0.966 against time.

🌎

Polar Amplification

The Arctic warms approximately 2.8× faster than the global mean, a well-documented feedback driven by ice–albedo dynamics and increased atmospheric moisture.

⚗

CO₂ Growth Rate Tripled

Annual CO₂ growth accelerated from ~0.9 ppm/yr in the 1960s to ~2.4 ppm/yr post-2000 — a 2.7× increase in the rate of accumulation.

📅

No Decade Cooler Than Prior

Every decade from the 1970s onward has been warmer than the previous. The 2000s–2010s transition showed the single largest decade-over-decade jump (+0.337°C).

🌿

Policy Has Not Bent the Curve

Kyoto (1997) and Paris (2015) mark clear milestones on the CO₂ chart, yet cumulative concentrations and annual growth rates continued rising through all periods.

📊

Statistical Robustness

All three primary trends show R² > 0.93. The linear model explains over 93% of variance in each variable — an exceptionally strong and consistent signal.
